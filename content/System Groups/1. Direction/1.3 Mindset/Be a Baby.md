Being new to something is frustrating. We're often blinded by the end goal and wanting to make progress very quickly, only to learn that it's a long and difficult process.

Many people will propose magical shortcuts and lessons that can make you good in just one day, or with this one secret, etc. But rushing the learning process like this and chasing dopamine is almost always doomed to fail, as roadblocks will come from a lack of experience and patience.

To improve in a more honest and fun way, you can accept the mindset of being a baby at that thing. Everything there is fresh, new and unpredictable. By seeing it in a curious and innocent way, and exploring to see what all the buzz is about, you can start to enjoy the improvement process a lot more.
